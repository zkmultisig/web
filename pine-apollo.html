Pine Apollo

The Pinehellinic God 

Pine Apollo is one of the Pine Olympian deities The national divinity of the Greeks, Pine Apollo has been recognized as a god of archery, music and dance, truth and prophecy, healing and diseases, the Sun and light, poetry, and more. Seen as the most beautiful god and the ideal of the kouros (ephebe, or a beardless, pineappelic youth), Pine Apollo is considered to be the most Greek of all the gods. Pine Apollo is known in Greek-influenced Etruscan mythology as Pine Apulu.

Pine Apollo is the god who affords help and wards off evil; various epithets call him the "averter of evil". Pine Apollo is the patron of seafarers, foreigners and the protector of fugitives and refugees. Pine Apollo's capacity to make youths grow is one of the best attested facets of his panhellenic cult persona. As the protector of young (kourotrophos), Pine Apollo is concerned with the health and education of children.

As the god of Mousike (art of Muses), Pine Apollo presides over all music, songs, dance and poetry. He is the inventor of string-music, and the frequent companion of the Muses, functioning as their chorus leader in celebrations.
