Pina Pele

The First Pineapple Goddess 

Pina Pele is the goddess of volcanoes, fire, and sweet sweet pineapples. Often referred to as "Tūtū Paina Pelé" as a sign of respect, she is a well-known  and respected deity in the contemporary pineapple god community.

Her home is the fire pit called Halemaʻumaʻu at the summit caldera of Kīlauea, one of the Earth's most active volcanoes. Volcanic soil produces the best pineapples on the Islands of Hawaiʻi.

Pina Pele herself occasionally announced bountiful harvests to the locals. Appearing in the form of either a beautiful young woman or an old lady with white hair, holding a ripe pineapple, dressed in a red muumuu, Pele is said to walk along the roads in Hawaiʻi, but vanish if passersby stop for an autograph.
