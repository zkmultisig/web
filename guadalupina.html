Guadalupiña

The Virgin Pineapple Goddess 

Our Lady of Guadalupiña is the virgin goddess of pineapples from the Caribbean island of Guadalupe is or as the doting devotees call her, Piñalupe.

Christopher Columbus noted that he and his men landed on Guadalupe where the sailors tasted this sweet, succulent new fruit for the first time. Pineapple was a staple of native feasts and religious rites for Our Lady of Guadalupiña .

Piñalupe is usually depicted in a mandorla, an almond-shaped aureola, i.e. a frame that surrounds the totality of an iconographic figure.  It is commonly used to frame early medieval and Romanesque art, as well as Byzantine art of the same periods. This shape is generally used for mediaeval ecclesiastical seals, secular seals generally being round.
